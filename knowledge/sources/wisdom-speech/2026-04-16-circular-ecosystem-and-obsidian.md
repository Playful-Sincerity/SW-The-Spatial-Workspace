## Pattern Observations

- The instinct is **structure-preserving visualization**, not force-directed graph chaos. Obsidian's graph view is the contrast — pretty but illegible. Markmap-style (hierarchical, radial) is the ideal because folders stay laid out.
- "Real-time" and "constantly updating" matters — he wants the visualizer to be a living mirror of the ecosystem state, not a snapshot.
- The Obsidian/VS Code question is genuine ambivalence, not a decision being made. He named both his current preference (VS Code) and his anticipated future move (Obsidian when it has everything). The "maybe for clients" lands as a realistic intermediate use — Obsidian as the consumer-friendly reading layer.
- The mention of BRAT (Beta Reviewers Auto-update Tool, used to install community Obsidian plugins not in the official store) suggests he's already done some Obsidian setup but hasn't integrated it into daily flow.

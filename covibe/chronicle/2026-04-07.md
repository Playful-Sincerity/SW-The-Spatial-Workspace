## 15:00 — [Pivot]

**What:** Wisdom reframed CoVibe's architecture from "shared context window" to "sessions that communicate with each other."
**Why:** "You don't need people to share context. You just want the conversations to talk to each other." This is simpler, more natural, and maps to how humans actually coordinate.
**Means:** The architecture shifts from trying to multiplex one context window to a message-passing protocol between independent sessions. Each person keeps their own Claude, sessions post/read coordination messages, orchestrator sees everything. Much simpler to build, more robust, and actually a better product.
